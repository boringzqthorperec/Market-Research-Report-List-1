<p><p>The hatchback aluminum alloy wheel market is primarily divided into two types: casting and forging. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into molds, allowing for intricate designs and efficient mass production, making it a cost-effective choice for many manufacturers. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping aluminum under high pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels with superior performance characteristics. Both methods cater to different consumer preferences, with casting focusing on affordability and design flexibility, while forging emphasizes durability and performance.</p></p>
