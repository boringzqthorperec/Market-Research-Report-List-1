<p><p>Meditech textiles are categorized into four primary market types. Woven fabrics are crafted by interlacing threads, offering durability and design versatility, ideal for applications like surgical gowns and drapes. Non-woven fabrics are made by bonding fibers together through mechanical, thermal, or chemical processes, providing cost-effective and lightweight options for disposables and filters. Knitted fabrics are produced by interlooping yarns, resulting in flexibility and comfort, suitable for clothing and support garments. Lastly, "Others" encompasses innovative materials like bio-engineered textiles and composites.</p></p>
