<p><p>The temperature probes/sensors market is divided into two primary types: non-contact and contact temperature sensors. Non-contact temperature sensors utilize infrared technology to measure temperature from a distance, making them ideal for moving objects or hazardous environments. In contrast, contact temperature sensors require direct physical contact with the object being measured, offering precise readings for solid, liquid, or gas samples. Both technologies cater to diverse applications across industries such as manufacturing, healthcare, and automotive, ensuring efficient temperature monitoring and control.</p></p>
