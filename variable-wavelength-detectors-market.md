<p><p>Variable wavelength detectors are critical in chromatography, enabling the analysis of different compounds based on their light absorption properties. UV-visible detectors operate by measuring absorbance at varying wavelengths, suitable for many organic compounds. Refractive index detectors measure changes in the refractive index to quantify substances. ELSD detectors provide sensitivity for non-volatile compounds, while fluorescence detectors utilize specific excitation wavelengths to detect fluorescent compounds. Other market types might include mass spectrometry detectors or conductance detectors, enhancing analytical capabilities across various applications.</p></p>
